# Dead by Daylight - Progression & Customization Systems

## Overview

Dead by Daylight features comprehensive progression systems that allow players to customize their characters, unlock new abilities, and develop their playstyle through various mechanics including the Bloodweb, perk system, and cosmetic customization.

## Bloodweb System

### Bloodpoint Economy

**Bloodpoint Sources:**
- **Match Performance**: Points earned based on actions
- **Daily Rituals**: Bonus objectives with bloodpoint rewards
- **Offerings**: Temporary bloodpoint multipliers
- **Events**: Special event bonuses and rewards

**Bloodpoint Categories:**
- **Boldness**: Chase and evasion actions (8,000 max)
- **Altruism**: Helping teammates actions (8,000 max)
- **Survival**: Escape and objective actions (8,000 max)
- **Objective**: Generator repair actions (8,000 max)
- **Total Maximum**: 32,000 bloodpoints per match

### Bloodweb Mechanics

**Node Types:**
- **Perk Nodes**: Unlock new perks for characters
- **Item Nodes**: Obtain consumable items
- **Add-on Nodes**: Unlock item and power enhancements
- **Offering Nodes**: Obtain match modifiers
- **Entity Nodes**: Block progression paths

**Bloodweb Strategy:**
- **Optimal Pathing**: Most efficient route to desired nodes
- **Entity Manipulation**: Guiding Entity to block unwanted paths
- **Priority Selection**: Choosing most valuable nodes first
- **Resource Management**: Balancing bloodpoint spending

### Prestige System

**Prestige Benefits:**
- **Prestige 1-3**: Bloody cosmetic items
- **Prestige 4-6**: Additional cosmetic variations
- **Prestige 7-9**: Unique cosmetic rewards
- **Prestige Legacy**: Special legacy cosmetics

**Prestige Costs:**
- **Bloodpoint Investment**: Significant bloodpoint requirements
- **Progression Reset**: Character level reset to 1
- **Perk Retention**: Teachable perks remain available
- **Item Loss**: All items and add-ons are lost

## Perk System

### Perk Acquisition

**Character-Specific Perks:**
- **Level 30, 35, 40**: Unlock teachable versions
- **Teachable Perks**: Available in other characters' bloodwebs
- **Perk Tiers**: I, II, III (increasing effectiveness)
- **Rarity Colors**: Yellow, Green, Purple

**Perk Rarities:**
- **Common (Yellow)**: Basic perk effects
- **Uncommon (Green)**: Enhanced effects
- **Rare (Purple)**: Strong effects with conditions
- **Very Rare (Pink)**: Powerful effects with drawbacks
- **Ultra Rare (Red)**: Game-changing effects

### Perk Categories & Synergies

**Survivor Perk Types:**
- **Information**: Aura reading and detection
- **Healing**: Medical and recovery benefits
- **Chase**: Evasion and looping assistance
- **Objective**: Generator and objective efficiency
- **Team Support**: Cooperative benefits
- **Stealth**: Concealment and quiet movement

**Killer Perk Types:**
- **Generator Defense**: Slowdown and regression
- **Chase Enhancement**: Tracking and pursuit
- **Information**: Survivor detection and location
- **Slowdown**: Game-wide pressure
- **Hex**: High-risk, high-reward totem perks
- **Utility**: Miscellaneous benefits

### Meta Perk Analysis

**Current Meta Perks (Survivor):**
- **Dead Hard**: Temporary invincibility dash
- **Borrowed Time**: Endurance for unhooked Survivors
- **Iron Will**: Reduced pain sounds
- **Spine Chill**: Killer detection warning
- **Adrenaline**: Endgame healing and speed

**Current Meta Perks (Killer):**
- **Barbecue & Chilli**: Aura reading after hooks
- **Pop Goes the Weasel**: Generator regression
- **Corrupt Intervention**: Early game generator block
- **Hex: Ruin**: Automatic generator regression
- **No Way Out**: Endgame gate block
