# Dead by Daylight - Lore & Game Elements

## Overview

Dead by Daylight features a rich lore and mythology that explains the game's mechanics, characters, and world. Understanding the lore enhances gameplay immersion and provides context for the game's systems and characters.

## The Entity & The Fog

### The Entity

**Nature and Purpose:**
- **Cosmic Being**: An ancient, god-like entity that feeds on human hope and despair
- **Realm Creator**: The Entity creates and controls the trial realms where matches occur
- **Game Master**: Orchestrates the deadly games between Killers and Survivors
- **Emotional Sustenance**: Feeds on the strong emotions generated during trials

**Entity Mechanics:**
- **Hook Sacrifices**: The Entity consumes Survivors through hook sacrifices
- **Bloodweb**: Entity's progression system for character development
- **Environmental Control**: Can block windows, create hooks, and manipulate the trial
- **Endgame Collapse**: Entity's final attempt to claim remaining Survivors

**Lore Significance:**
- **Mysterious Origins**: Unknown true nature and origins
- **Manipulative Nature**: Plays both sides for maximum emotional output
- **Psychological Warfare**: Creates scenarios that generate strongest emotions
- **Eternal Cycle**: Endless repetition of trials for sustenance

### The Fog

**Nature of The Fog:**
- **Interdimensional Realm**: The space between worlds where trials occur
- **Entity's Domain**: Controlled and shaped by The Entity
- **Shifting Landscape**: Maps are manifestations within The Fog
- **Psychological Manifestation**: Reflects fears and memories of participants

**Gameplay Role:**
- **Atmosphere Creation**: Horror ambiance and tension
- **Visibility Limitation**: Strategic gameplay element
- **Audio Enhancement**: Sound carries differently in The Fog
- **Immersion Factor**: Creates sense of isolation and dread

**Lore Implications:**
- **Endless Expanse**: Infinite possibilities for trial locations
- **Memory Integration**: Incorporates elements from participants' pasts
- **Entity Manipulation**: Can be shaped and altered by The Entity
- **Escape Difficulty**: Navigating The Fog is nearly impossible

## Character Lore & Backstories

### Original Survivors

#### Dwight Fairfield
**Background:** Office worker who excelled at management but lacked confidence
**Disappearance:** Vanished during a company retreat in the woods
**Personality:** Natural leader who struggles with self-doubt
**Lore Significance:** Represents corporate mundanity contrasted with supernatural horror

#### Meg Thomas
**Background:** Track star dealing with her mother's illness
**Disappearance:** Last seen running through the woods during training
**Personality:** Determined athlete with family loyalty
**Lore Significance:** Physical prowess and determination in face of supernatural threat

#### Claudette Morel
**Background:** Botany student with exceptional medical knowledge
**Disappearance:** Went missing while collecting plant samples
**Personality:** Compassionate healer and scientist
**Lore Significance:** Scientific rationality meets supernatural horror

#### Jake Park
**Background:** Wealthy heir who rejected his family's corporate lifestyle
**Disappearance:** Vanished while living off-grid in the wilderness
**Personality:** Independent survivalist with anti-establishment views
**Lore Significance:** Natural survival skills versus supernatural threats

### Original Killers

#### The Trapper (Evan MacMillan)
**Background:** Heir to MacMillan Estate who became obsessed with "hunting" workers
**Corruption:** The Entity twisted his already violent tendencies
**Methods:** Uses bear traps from his family's mining operations
**Lore Significance:** Represents corporate exploitation turning violent

#### The Wraith (Philip Ojomo)
**Background:** Junkyard worker who discovered his employer's murderous activities
**Corruption:** Forced to participate in murders, then consumed by guilt
**Methods:** Uses a bell that once signaled deaths in the junkyard
**Lore Significance:** Tragic figure consumed by circumstances beyond his control

#### The Nurse (Sally Smithson)
**Background:** War nurse who became obsessed with easing patients' suffering
**Corruption:** Began mercy killing patients, believing she was helping
**Methods:** Uses supernatural abilities gained from her experiences
**Lore Significance:** Twisted compassion and medical authority

#### The Hillbilly (Max Thompson Jr.)
**Background:** Deformed child kept hidden by ashamed parents
**Corruption:** Years of isolation and abuse created violent tendencies
**Methods:** Uses chainsaw from family farm
**Lore Significance:** Horror of isolation and family shame

## Game Mechanics Lore Explanations

### Trial Structure

**Purpose of Trials:**
- **Emotional Harvest**: Generate hope, fear, and despair for The Entity
- **Endless Cycle**: Trials repeat indefinitely with different participants
- **Psychological Testing**: Push characters to their emotional limits
- **Entertainment**: The Entity enjoys the spectacle of the hunt

**Generator Mechanics:**
- **Power Sources**: Provide energy to open escape routes
- **Entity Manipulation**: The Entity allows this escape possibility
- **Hope Generation**: Creates the possibility of escape for emotional payoff
- **Strategic Element**: Part of The Entity's game design

### Hook Sacrifice System

**Sacrifice Process:**
- **Entity Consumption**: The Entity literally consumes sacrificed Survivors
- **Emotional Peak**: Maximum despair at moment of sacrifice
- **Cycle Continuation**: Sacrificed Survivors are eventually returned for new trials
- **Psychological Impact**: Creates trauma and fear in remaining Survivors

**Struggle Phase:**
- **Will to Live**: Survivors fighting against inevitable consumption
- **Entity's Enjoyment**: The Entity enjoys the struggle and desperation
- **Team Dynamics**: Opportunity for rescue creates hope and altruism
- **Emotional Rollercoaster**: Hope of rescue versus despair of sacrifice

### Perk System Lore

**Character Abilities:**
- **Innate Talents**: Natural skills and abilities characters possessed in life
- **Entity Enhancement**: The Entity amplifies these abilities for the trials
- **Psychological Traits**: Perks reflect characters' personalities and experiences
- **Learning Process**: Characters can learn from each other through teachable perks

**Bloodweb Progression:**
- **Entity's Reward System**: The Entity provides progression as incentive
- **Character Development**: Survivors and Killers grow stronger through trials
- **Addiction to Power**: The progression system keeps participants engaged
- **Manipulation Tool**: The Entity uses progression to control behavior

## Map Lore & Environments

### Realm Types

**MacMillan Estate:**
- **The Trapper's Domain**: Reflects Evan MacMillan's family business
- **Mining Theme**: Underground mines and industrial facilities
- **Family Legacy**: Locations from The Trapper's past
- **Corporate Horror**: Exploitation and industrial danger

**Autohaven Wreckers:**
- **The Wraith's Domain**: Based on Philip Ojomo's junkyard workplace
- **Urban Decay**: Abandoned vehicles and industrial waste
- **Murder Site**: Location of the crimes he witnessed
- **Hidden Horrors**: Secrets buried in the wreckage

**Coldwind Farm:**
- **The Hillbilly's Domain**: Max Thompson's family farm
- **Rural Horror**: Isolated farm buildings and fields
- **Family Secrets**: Locations of his abuse and isolation
- **Agricultural Terror**: Farming equipment as weapons

**Crotus Prenn Asylum:**
- **The Nurse's Domain**: Sally Smithson's workplace
- **Medical Horror**: Abandoned psychiatric facility
- **Mercy Killing Site**: Location of her "compassionate" murders
- **Institutional Terror**: Horror of medical authority

## Morality & Themes

### Moral Ambiguity

**Killer Sympathy:**
- **Tragic Origins**: Many Killers were victims before becoming monsters
- **Entity Corruption**: The Entity twists already damaged individuals
- **Forced Participation**: Some Killers are compelled against their will
- **Moral Complexity**: Blurred lines between victim and perpetrator

**Survivor Flaws:**
- **Imperfect Heroes**: Survivors have their own flaws and mistakes
- **Selfish Moments**: Sometimes prioritize survival over teamwork
- **Moral Choices**: Decisions about who to save and when
- **Psychological Damage**: Trauma from repeated trials

### Recurring Themes

**Hope vs Despair:**
- **Cycle of Emotions**: The Entity feeds on the transition between hope and despair
- **Escape Possibility**: Always present but difficult to achieve
- **Psychological Warfare**: Manipulation of emotions for sustenance
- **Human Resilience**: Ability to maintain hope in hopeless situations

**Isolation vs Community:**
- **Individual Survival**: Sometimes necessary to prioritize self
- **Team Cooperation**: Often essential for group survival
- **Trust Issues**: Difficulty trusting strangers in life-or-death situations
- **Shared Trauma**: Bonds formed through shared experiences

**Nature of Evil:**
- **Supernatural Evil**: The Entity as cosmic malevolence
- **Human Evil**: Killers who were already violent before Entity
- **Circumstantial Evil**: Ordinary people twisted by circumstances
- **Moral Responsibility**: Questions of choice versus compulsion
