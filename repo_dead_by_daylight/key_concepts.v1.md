# Dead By Daylight - Key Concepts & Game Mechanics

## Overview

**Dead by Daylight** is an asymmetric multiplayer horror game developed by Behaviour Interactive where 4 Survivors face off against 1 Killer in a deadly game of cat and mouse.

**Resources:**
- Official Website: https://deadbydaylight.com/
- Official Wiki: https://deadbydaylight.fandom.com/

---

## Core Gameplay Mechanics

### 1. Asymmetric Gameplay
- **4 vs 1 Format**: Four Survivors vs One Killer
- **Different Objectives**: Survivors must escape, Killer must sacrifice Survivors
- **Perspective Difference**: Survivors play in third-person, Killer plays in first-person

### 2. The Trial Structure
- **Maps**: Procedurally generated environments with randomized elements
- **Objectives**: Survivors repair generators to power exit gates
- **Time Limit**: The Entity (game mechanic) gradually closes the trial

### 3. Core Mechanics
- **Generators**: 5 generators must be repaired to power exit gates
- **Hooks**: Killers must hook Survivors 3 times to sacrifice them to The Entity
- **Health States**: Healthy → Injured → Dying (on ground)
- **Escape Routes**: Exit gates, hatch (when only one Survivor remains)

## Character Roles

### Survivors
**Objective**: Repair generators and escape the trial

**Key Abilities:**
- **Repairing**: Fix generators to power exit gates
- **Healing**: Mend injured Survivors
- **Saving**: Rescue hooked teammates
- **Stealth**: Hide from the Killer using environment
- **Perks**: Unique abilities that provide advantages

**Notable Survivors:**
- Dwight Fairfield (Leader)
- Meg Thomas (Athlete)
- Claudette Morel (Medic)
- Jake Park (Saboteur)
- Leon S. Kennedy (Resident Evil)
- Cheryl Mason (Silent Hill)

### Killers
**Objective**: Hunt and sacrifice all Survivors

**Key Abilities:**
- **Chasing**: Pursue and damage Survivors
- **Special Powers**: Unique abilities for each Killer
- **Hook Management**: Strategic placement of hooks
- **Perks**: Killer-specific advantages

**Notable Killers:**
- The Trapper (Bear traps)
- The Wraith (Invisibility)
- The Hillbilly (Chainsaw sprint)
- The Nurse (Teleportation)
- The Ghost Face (Stealth and marking)
- The Pig (Reverse bear traps)

## Progression & Customization

### 1. Bloodweb System
- **Bloodpoints**: Currency earned from matches
- **Progression**: Unlock new perks, items, and add-ons
- **Prestige System**: Reset progression for cosmetic rewards

### 2. Perks System
- **Unique Perks**: Each character has 3 unique perks
- **Teachable Perks**: Unlock perks for other characters
- **Perk Tiers**: I, II, III (increasing effectiveness)

### 3. Items & Add-ons
- **Survivor Items**: Toolboxes, med-kits, flashlights, maps
- **Killer Add-ons**: Enhance special powers
- **Offerings**: Modify trial conditions

## Strategies & Gameplay Tips

### Survivor Strategies
1. **Teamwork**: Coordinate generator repairs and rescues
2. **Stealth**: Avoid detection through careful movement
3. **Looping**: Use environment to evade the Killer
4. **Resource Management**: Use items strategically
5. **Awareness**: Monitor Killer location and teammate status

### Killer Strategies
1. **Patrol Routes**: Efficiently check generators
2. **Pressure Management**: Keep Survivors on defensive
3. **Hook Strategy**: Control hook placement and timing
4. **Power Usage**: Master unique Killer abilities
5. **Mind Games**: Predict Survivor movements

## Important Game Elements

### The Entity
- **Lore**: Mysterious force that controls the trials
- **Mechanics**: Blocks windows, creates hooks, ends trials
- **Bloodweb**: Entity progression system

### The Fog
- **Atmosphere**: Creates horror ambiance
- **Gameplay**: Limits visibility, adds tension
- **Lore**: Mysterious realm where trials occur

### Morality System
- **Bloodpoints**: Rewards for positive actions
- **Emblems**: Performance ratings (Benevolent, Evader, etc.)
- **Ranking**: Competitive progression system
