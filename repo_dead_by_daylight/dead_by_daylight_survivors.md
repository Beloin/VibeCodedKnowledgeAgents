# Dead by Daylight - Survivors Guide

## Overview

Survivors are the hunted players in Dead by Daylight, working together to repair generators and escape the trial while avoiding capture by the Killer. Each Survivor has unique perks and playstyles that contribute to team success.

## Survivor Role & Objectives

### Primary Objectives
- **Generator Repair**: Repair 5 generators to power exit gates
- **Team Survival**: Ensure as many teammates escape as possible
- **Resource Management**: Use items and perks strategically
- **Killer Evasion**: Avoid detection and capture

### Secondary Objectives
- **Rescue Teammates**: Save hooked Survivors
- **Heal Injuries**: Maintain team health states
- **Sabotage**: Remove hooks and disrupt Killer plans
- **Distraction**: Occupy Killer attention

## Core Survivor Mechanics

### Movement & Navigation

**Movement Speeds:**
- **Walking**: 100% speed (4.0 m/s)
- **Running**: 150% speed (6.0 m/s)
- **Crouching**: 90% speed (3.6 m/s)
- **Injured**: 95% speed (3.8 m/s)

**Environmental Interactions:**
- **Vaulting**: Quick (2.0s) vs Slow (3.0s) vaults
- **Pallet Dropping**: Stuns Killer if timed correctly
- **Locker Hiding**: Complete concealment but vulnerable
- **Generator Repair**: Skill check-based progression

### Health States & Recovery

**Health State Progression:**
1. **Healthy** → **Injured** (Killer attack)
2. **Injured** → **Dying** (Second Killer attack)
3. **Dying** → **Recovery** (Self-recovery or teammate help)
4. **Hooked** → **Struggle** → **Sacrifice**

**Healing Times:**
- **Self-Care**: 32 seconds (base)
- **Teammate Healing**: 16 seconds (base)
- **Med-Kit Healing**: Varies by item quality

## Survivor Perk Categories

### Information Perks
**Purpose:** Gather intelligence about Killer location and teammate status

**Key Perks:**
- **Spine Chill**: Warning when Killer looks in your direction
- **Kindred**: See Killer and Survivor auras when someone is hooked
- **Alert**: See Killer aura when they break objects
- **Dark Sense**: See Killer aura after completing generators

### Healing Perks
**Purpose:** Improve healing efficiency and self-preservation

**Key Perks:**
- **We'll Make It**: 100% faster healing after unhooking
- **Autodidact**: Skill checks grant healing progression
- **Inner Strength**: Heal in lockers after cleansing totems
- **Desperate Measures**: Faster healing and unhooking

### Chase & Evasion Perks
**Purpose:** Improve survival during Killer chases

**Key Perks:**
- **Dead Hard**: Dash forward to avoid attacks
- **Lithe**: Gain speed boost after fast vault
- **Balanced Landing**: Reduce stagger and gain speed after fall
- **Windows of Opportunity**: See pallet and window auras

### Objective Perks
**Purpose:** Improve generator repair and objective completion

**Key Perks:**
- **Resilience**: Faster action speed when injured
- **Spine Chill**: Faster action speed when Killer is nearby
- **This Is Not Happening**: Larger skill check zones when injured
- **Deja Vu**: See generator auras and repair faster

### Team Support Perks
**Purpose:** Help teammates and coordinate efforts

**Key Perks:**
- **We're Gonna Live Forever**: Bonus bloodpoints for protection
- **Borrowed Time**: Endurance for unhooked Survivors
- **Guardian**: See Killer aura after unhooking
- **Breakout**: Help carried Survivors wiggle free faster

## Advanced Survivor Strategies

**Optimal Repair Strategy:**
- **Split Pressure**: Work on different generators
- **Three-Gen Strategy**: Avoid clustering final generators
- **Regression Prevention**: Stop repairs before Killer arrives
- **Toolbox Efficiency**: Use charges strategically

- **Blast Mine**: Trap generators for stuns

- **Prove Thyself**: Faster cooperative repairs

## Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

### Positioning Errors
- **Over-Repairing**: Multiple Survivors on one generator
- **Poor Hook Awareness**: Unhooking without checking for Killer
- **Generator Clustering**: Creating easy patrol routes
- **Open Space Running**: Leaving scratch marks unnecessarily

### Chase Mistakes
- **Panic Vaulting**: Wasting pallets early
- **Poor Resource Management**: Using all pallets in one area
- **Predictable Patterns**: Repeating same loop patterns
- **Over-Extending**: Staying in chase too long

### Team Coordination Issues
- **Selfish Play**: Ignoring hooked teammates
- **Poor Communication**: Not sharing information
- **Generator Neglect**: Focusing only on chases
- **Endgame Confusion**: Uncoordinated escape attempts

## Advanced Tips & Tricks

### Mind Games
- **Reverse Psychology**: Doing the unexpected
- **Baiting Attacks**: Intentionally exposing yourself
- **Fake Healing**: Pretending to heal to bait Killer
- **Direction Faking**: Running one way then changing

### Environmental Exploitation
- **Map Knowledge**: Knowing every loop and hiding spot
- **Audio Cues**: Using sounds to track Killer location
- **Lighting Conditions**: Using shadows for concealment
- **Terrain Features**: Using elevation and obstacles

### Psychological Warfare
- **Confidence Display**: Appearing unafraid to intimidate Killer
- **Bold Actions**: Taking risks to pressure Killer
- **Team Morale**: Keeping teammates motivated
- **Killer Frustration**: Making the Killer make mistakes

## Conclusion

Mastering Survivor gameplay requires a combination of mechanical skill, strategic thinking, and teamwork. Successful Survivors understand when to be aggressive and when to be stealthy, how to manage resources effectively, and how to work with their team to achieve victory. Continuous learning and adaptation to different Killers and situations are key to becoming an expert Survivor.
