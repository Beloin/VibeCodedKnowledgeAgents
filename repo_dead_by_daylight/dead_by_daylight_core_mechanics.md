# Dead by Daylight - Core Game Mechanics

## Overview

Dead by Daylight is an asymmetric multiplayer horror game developed by Behaviour Interactive that pits 4 Survivors against 1 Killer in a deadly game of cat and mouse. The game's core mechanics create a unique blend of horror, strategy, and teamwork.

## Asymmetric Gameplay Design

### 4 vs 1 Format
- **Survivor Team**: Four players working cooperatively
- **Killer Role**: One player hunting the Survivors
- **Different Objectives**: Survivors must escape, Killer must sacrifice Survivors
- **Perspective Difference**: Survivors play in third-person, Killer plays in first-person

### Game Balance Philosophy
- **Survivor Advantages**: Numbers, cooperation, stealth capabilities
- **Killer Advantages**: Power, map control, special abilities
- **Dynamic Balance**: Game mechanics adjust based on match progress

## The Trial Structure

### Match Progression
- **Preparation Phase**: Character selection and loadout setup
- **Trial Start**: All players spawn in random locations
- **Objective Phase**: Survivors repair generators while Killer hunts
- **Endgame**: Exit gates powered or hatch escape

### Time Management
- **Entity Progression**: The Entity gradually closes the trial
- **Generator Timers**: Each generator takes approximately 80 seconds to repair
- **Hook Stages**: Survivors progress through hook states over time

## Core Gameplay Mechanics

### Generator Repair System

**Basic Mechanics:**
- **5 Generators Required**: Must repair all 5 to power exit gates
- **Skill Checks**: Random skill checks appear during repair
- **Repair Speed**: Base speed of 1 charge per second
- **Toolbox Bonuses**: Items can increase repair speed

**Advanced Generator Mechanics:**
- **Regression**: Killers can damage generators to regress progress
- **Overcharge**: Special Killer perks that create difficult skill checks
- **Prove Thyself**: Survivor perk for cooperative repair bonuses

### Hook Sacrifice System

**Hook Stages:**
1. **First Hook**: Survivor enters struggle phase
2. **Second Hook**: Progress to next stage
3. **Third Hook**: Sacrifice to The Entity

**Hook Mechanics:**
- **Struggle Phase**: Button mashing to delay sacrifice
- **Rescue Timing**: Strategic timing for unhooking teammates
- **Hook Placement**: Strategic positioning by Killers
- **Camping vs Patrolling**: Killer strategy decisions

### Health States & Healing

**Survivor Health States:**
- **Healthy**: Full health, can take one hit
- **Injured**: Reduced speed, can be downed by next hit
- **Dying**: On ground, requires teammate rescue
- **Hooked**: On hook, progressing toward sacrifice

**Healing Mechanics:**
- **Self-Heal**: Base 16 seconds without items
- **Teammate Healing**: Base 16 seconds
- **Med-Kit Bonuses**: Items reduce healing time
- **Healing Perks**: Various perks affect healing speed and efficiency

### Escape Routes

**Primary Escape Methods:**
- **Exit Gates**: Two gates powered by 5 generators
- **Hatch**: Appears when only one Survivor remains
- **Key Escape**: Using a key to open hatch early

**Escape Mechanics:**
- **Gate Opening**: 20-second animation to open exit gates
- **Hatch Spawn**: Random location when conditions met
- **Endgame Collapse**: Timer starts after first gate opens

## Advanced Mechanics

### Chase Mechanics

**Looping Strategies:**
- **Pallet Loops**: Using pallets to create safe zones
- **Window Vaults**: Quick vaults vs slow vaults
- **Mind Games**: Predicting Killer movements
- **Dead Hard**: Perk for temporary invincibility

**Killer Chase Tools:**
- **Bloodlust**: Speed boost after prolonged chase
- **Lunge Attacks**: Extended reach for hits
- **Special Powers**: Killer-specific chase abilities

### Stealth & Detection

**Survivor Stealth:**
- **Crouching**: Reduced movement speed, quieter
- **Urban Evasion**: Perk for faster crouch walking
- **Spine Chill**: Perk that warns of Killer approach
- **Darkness**: Using shadows and environment for cover

**Killer Detection:**
- **Terror Radius**: Audio cue of Killer proximity
- **Red Stain**: Visual indicator of Killer direction
- **Scratch Marks**: Temporary trails left by running Survivors
- **Aura Reading**: Perks that reveal Survivor locations

### Item Management

**Survivor Items:**
- **Toolboxes**: Generator repair and sabotage
- **Med-Kits**: Healing and self-care
- **Flashlights**: Blinding and stunning Killers
- **Maps**: Revealing objectives and items
- **Keys**: Hatch opening and aura reading

**Killer Add-ons:**
- **Power Enhancements**: Modify special abilities
- **Duration Extenders**: Increase power duration
- **Cooldown Reducers**: Decrease ability cooldowns
- **Special Effects**: Unique modifications to powers

## Game Progression Systems

### Bloodpoint Economy

**Bloodpoint Categories:**
- **Boldness**: Chase and evasion points
- **Altruism**: Helping teammates points
- **Survival**: Escape and objective points
- **Objective**: Generator repair points

**Bloodpoint Multipliers:**
- **Offerings**: Temporary bloodpoint bonuses
- **Events**: Special event multipliers
- **Daily Rituals**: Bonus objectives

### Ranking System

**Rank Tiers:**
- **Rank 20-16**: Beginner ranks
- **Rank 15-11**: Intermediate ranks
- **Rank 10-6**: Advanced ranks
- **Rank 5-1**: Expert ranks
- **Iridescent**: Highest rank tier

**Rank Progression:**
- **Pips**: Earned based on performance
- **Safety Pips**: Prevent rank loss
- **Double Pips**: Exceptional performance
- **Rank Reset**: Monthly rank reset

## Environmental Interactions

### Map Elements

**Interactive Objects:**
- **Pallets**: Can be dropped to stun Killers
- **Windows**: Can be vaulted for quick escapes
- **Lockers**: Hiding spots for Survivors
- **Totems**: Hex totems for Killer perks
- **Generators**: Primary objectives

**Environmental Hazards:**
- **Traps**: Killer-placed hazards
- **Environmental Killers**: Special map hazards
- **The Entity**: Blocks windows and creates obstacles

### Audio Cues

**Important Sounds:**
- **Terror Radius**: Killer proximity indicator
- **Generator Sounds**: Repair progress audio
- **Injury Sounds**: Survivor pain indicators
- **Hook Sounds**: Sacrifice progression
- **Footsteps**: Movement detection

## Strategic Considerations

### Resource Management

**Survivor Resources:**
- **Item Charges**: Limited uses for items
- **Pallet Resources**: Finite number of pallets
- **Health States**: Limited recovery options
- **Time Management**: Balancing objectives and survival

**Killer Resources:**
- **Power Cooldowns**: Managing ability usage
- **Hook Availability**: Strategic hook placement
- **Map Pressure**: Controlling multiple areas
- **Time Investment**: Efficient chase management

### Risk vs Reward

**High-Risk Actions:**
- **Generator Rush**: Fast repairs but high visibility
- **Bold Saves**: Dangerous unhook attempts
- **Aggressive Loops**: Extended chases
- **Item Usage**: Consuming limited resources

**Conservative Play:**
- **Stealth Approach**: Slow but safe progress
- **Team Coordination**: Waiting for opportunities
- **Resource Conservation**: Saving items for endgame
- **Safe Unhooks**: Waiting for Killer to leave area

## Conclusion

Understanding Dead by Daylight's core mechanics is essential for both Survivor and Killer gameplay. The asymmetric nature creates unique strategic challenges that require mastery of multiple systems working in tandem. Successful players must balance mechanical skill with strategic decision-making and adaptability to changing match conditions.
